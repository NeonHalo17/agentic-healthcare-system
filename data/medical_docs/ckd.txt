Disease: Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Overview:
Chronic Kidney Disease is a gradual loss of kidney function over time. It can progress to kidney failure if left untreated.

Symptoms:
- Fatigue
- Swelling in the legs and ankles
- High blood pressure
- Loss of appetite
- Nausea
- Decreased urine output
- Muscle cramps

Risk Factors:
- Diabetes
- Hypertension
- Family history of kidney disease
- Smoking
- Obesity

Diagnosis:
- Blood creatinine test
- eGFR
- Urine protein test
- Kidney ultrasound

Treatment:
- Blood pressure control
- ACE inhibitors or ARBs
- Blood sugar management
- Low protein diet
- Dialysis in advanced stages
- Kidney transplantation

Lifestyle Recommendations:
- Reduce salt intake
- Exercise regularly
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid smoking
